About Benin

The Heart of West Africa

Benin is a culturally rich and historically significant country in West Africa, often referred to as the cradle of the Vodun culture, which influences traditions far beyond its borders.

Vodun Days Celebration

Every year, Vodun Days (January 10th) bring together thousands of people from around the world in Ouidah to celebrate spirituality, heritage, and identity. This openness to culture and dialogue makes Benin a unique place for intercultural exchange.

Benin is also a land of youth creativity and innovation. Events like We Love Eya highlight the energy of young Beninese through music, arts, fashion, and entrepreneurship, reinforcing the country's growing creative economy.

Heritage & Modernity

With iconic sites such as the Royal Palaces of Abomey (UNESCO World Heritage), the historic Door of No Return, and the modern transformation of cities like Cotonou, Benin stands at the crossroads of tradition and modernity.

Opportunities for Youth Leadership

This environment offers young leaders a powerful space to develop leadership, engage communities, and create sustainable impact, making Benin a fertile ground for organizations like AIESEC to thrive.
